You: I'm heading to Tokyo in mid-November for 10 days. What will the weather be like?

Assistant: Mid-November in Tokyo is one of the nicer times to visit -- autumn foliage is usually at its peak. To give you an accurate forecast I need your exact dates. Do you have them?

You: November 12 to 22.

[tool call: get_weather(city=Tokyo, start_date=2025-11-12)]
[result: avg_high=16C, avg_low=8C, precipitation_chance=20%, conditions=Mostly clear, occasional light rain]

Assistant: Here is what the forecast shows for your dates:

- Highs around 15-17C, lows around 7-9C -- chilly but not cold.
- Mostly clear with a 20% chance of light rain on a couple of days (common in November).
- Humidity is low in November, so it feels crisper than the numbers suggest.

This is a comfortable range for walking -- you will not need a heavy winter coat, but you will want layers. Want me to put together a packing list based on this?

You: Yes, that would be great.

Assistant: Packing list for 10 days in Tokyo in mid-November:

Clothing:
- 3-4 mid-layer tops (long sleeve, light sweater)
- 1 warmer sweater or fleece for evenings -- temperatures drop noticeably after dark
- A light-to-medium weight jacket (not a winter coat -- a good wool or synthetic layer is enough)
- Comfortable walking shoes with good soles -- Tokyo involves a lot of walking and the subway involves a lot of stairs
- 1 pair of smart-casual shoes if you plan on nicer restaurants
- Slip-on shoes are practical -- many traditional sites and some ryokan require removing footwear

Rain:
- A compact fold-up umbrella (or pick one up at a convenience store in Tokyo for a few hundred yen -- they stock excellent ones)
- Light waterproof layer is optional but useful if you plan outdoor parks days

General:
- Power adapter (Japan uses Type A plugs, 100V -- most modern devices handle this fine)
- IC card top-up for transit (Suica or Pasmo) -- load it at the airport
- Cash -- Japan remains heavily cash-based outside central tourist areas
- A small day bag for walking; lockers are available at most major stations for larger bags

You: Will it be warm enough for Nikko in late November?

Assistant: Nikko is about 120km north of Tokyo and sits at higher elevation, so it runs 3-5C cooler than central Tokyo. On your last few days (November 20-22) you might see highs of 10-12C in Nikko and lows close to freezing overnight.

If you are going for the day -- which is the most common approach -- you will be fine with the layers already on your packing list. Add a thermal base layer if you run cold. The autumn colours at Nikko typically peak slightly earlier than Tokyo (late October to mid-November), so by the 20th the foliage may be past peak but the temples and cedar forests are still worth the trip.

You: Thanks. Any suggestions for getting there?

Assistant: The standard route is the Tobu Nikko Line from Asakusa station in Tokyo -- about 2 hours on the limited express (Spacia or Revaty trains). It is significantly cheaper than taking a Shinkansen to Utsunomiya and connecting, and the Tobu trains run directly to Nikko station.

A day trip ticket (Tobu Nikko All Area Pass) covers the round trip plus local buses within Nikko, which is useful since the main shrine complex is a 30-minute bus ride from the station.

You: Perfect, this is really helpful.

Assistant: You are all set. November is a great month for both Tokyo and Nikko -- the cooler air and autumn colours make it one of the most scenic times of year. Let me know if you want help planning the Tokyo days themselves.
