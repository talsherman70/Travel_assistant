Assistant: Here is a relaxed 5-day plan grouped by area to minimise transit time:

Day 1 -- Eixample (Architecture day)
Morning: Sagrada Familia -- book a tower ticket in advance, allow 2-2.5 hours.
Afternoon: Walk Passeig de Gracia to see Casa Batllo and Casa Mila (La Pedrera) facades; the street itself is worth the stroll.
Evening: Dinner in Eixample -- the neighbourhood has a high density of well-regarded restaurants. Look for a table at a spot with a blackboard menu (usually signals fresh daily dishes).

Day 2 -- Gothic Quarter and El Born
Morning: Gothic Quarter on foot -- the Barri Gotic is best before 10am before tour groups arrive. Cathedral, Pont del Bisbe, Placa Reial.
Afternoon: Cross into El Born -- Palau de la Musica Catalana (book ahead), then wander the streets around it.
Evening: El Born has a strong food scene; pintxos bars along Carrer del Parlament are a good option for a lighter evening.

Day 3 -- Gracia and Park Guell
Morning: Park Guell -- go early (tickets are timed, buy online). The monumental zone with the mosaic terrace is the highlight.
Afternoon: Walk down into Gracia neighbourhood -- local, quieter, good for coffee and browsing independent shops.
Evening: Back to Eixample or try a restaurant in Gracia itself.

Day 4 -- Barceloneta and waterfront
Morning: Barceloneta market for a slow breakfast, then walk the waterfront to the Olympic Port.
Afternoon: Montjuic by cable car or funicular -- Fundacio Joan Miro if contemporary art interests you, or just the castle and views.
Evening: Seafood dinner in Barceloneta. Note: I cannot name specific restaurants without live data, but the blocks one street back from the beach tend to have better value than the beachfront row.

Day 5 -- Buffer and revisit
Use this day to revisit anything from earlier in the trip, do a cooking class (widely available in the Gothic Quarter), or take a half-day trip to Montserrat (about 1 hour by train from Placa Espanya).

A few practical notes:
- Book Sagrada Familia, Park Guell, and Palau de la Musica well in advance -- they sell out.
- The T-Casual metro card (10 trips) is the most economical way to get around.
- Late September weather is reliable, but pack a light layer for evenings.
